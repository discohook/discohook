## The Issue

Recently, an exploit was brought to my attention whereby a user with sufficient permissions to create components could in essence assign a role to themselves higher than they should otherwise be able to access in the hierarchy due to an oversight in how flows check permissions. The issue was resolved as soon as possible.

## Timeline

- June 6: I am first alerted to the issue in private by a member of the support server. I begin investigating and researching a solution immediately.
- June 14: Another member makes a public support post about how they were affected by either a staff member or compromised account exploiting the issue. After determining from further correspondence that the root of their problem was this exploit, I move conversation to a private thread to limit exposure.
- June 16, AM: A patch is deployed. I continue correspondence with the administrators of the affected server.
- June 23: User-facing tools are added to the bot to help identify setup problems.

## What Changed

We have introduced the concept of "responsible users", which you can read more about [here](discohook://guide/troubleshooting/responsibility). The bot will now cease execution of flows if it encounters an action that the responsible user may not be able to perform on their own.

## Why are my attachments showing "File Missing"? / Why doesn't my welcomer/flow show the files I added?

This happens when you refresh the page or load a backup with attachments in it. When files are added to the message from your computer, they are only stored temporarily within your browser.

If you want to make your attached files persist in backups, share links, or just refreshing the page, you can choose to upload it to a filehosting service. 

## How do I fix "Some filenames are used more than once"?

Most of the time, this happens after you accidentally add a file twice, and you can simply remove the duplicate file. However, if you are pasting files from your clipboard and want to give them different names, just click the pencil icon to rename them.

## What does "Convert References" mean?

When you use an attachment in an embed or display component (such as a gallery or thumbnail), it becomes referenced by that embed or component. This also means it will not be shown in the normal attachment gallery (above embeds, below text, for a standard message).

If you have uploaded the attachment to a filehosting service, it becomes "remote", meaning Discohook is no longer using the file stored on your computer. Attachments that are both remote and referenced do not need to be attachments at all. The embeds or components that reference them can simply use the plain URL instead.

If you see this button in the attachment editing menu, it is recommended to press it to free up your attachment limit. If you do not, don't worry, Discohook will handle the references on its own.
